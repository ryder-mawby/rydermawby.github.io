About

I am an MA student in Economics at California State University, Long Beach (expected December 2026) and a UC Berkeley alumnus (BA Political Economy, 2023).

My research applies structural and reduced-form econometric methods to illicit markets, platform economies, and labor market outcomes. I work primarily with large-scale microdata, combining data construction pipelines with high-dimensional estimation techniques.

I program in Stata, R, and Python, with a focus on PPML gravity models, panel data methods, and causal inference.

Research

Digital Illicit Trade and the Geography of Retail Drug Markets
MA Thesis · 2025–Present

Applies a structural gravity model to darknet cryptomarket trade using 354,000+ listing-level observations. Finds that standard gravity facilitation variables invert — contiguity, common language, and colonial ties suppress trade — with effects operating through the intensive margin and driven by enforcement coordination.

Paper Code
EU Membership and Bilateral Trade
2026

PPML gravity model with three-way fixed effects using CEPII data. Finds EU membership increases bilateral trade by ~12%, robust across specifications.

Labor Supply Responses to Proposition 22
2026 · Co-authored

Difference-in-differences analysis using CPS microdata. Finds an ~11% decline in gig self-employment following policy implementation.
