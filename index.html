About

I am an MA student in Economics at California State University, Long Beach (completing December 2026) and a UC Berkeley alumnus (BA Political Economy, 2023, Regents' Scholar). My research applies structural econometric methods to illicit markets, with a focus on darknet cryptomarket data, bilateral trade flows, and the geography of enforcement.

My current work uses listing-level data from the Hikari darknet archive — obtained through direct researcher correspondence with Nicolas Christin (CMU) and David Décary-Hétu (Université de Montréal) — to estimate gravity models of illicit drug trade. I program in Stata and R.

Working Paper
Digital Illicit Trade and the Geography of Retail Drug Markets: Evidence from Cryptomarkets Using a Gravity Framework
Working Paper  ·  April 2026

Using 354,000+ listing-level observations from 10 darknet cryptomarkets collapsed to a bilateral country-pair × drug-type × month panel, I estimate PPML gravity models with high-dimensional fixed effects. Distance elasticity: approximately −2.05. Contrary to every licit-goods gravity study, contiguity, common official language, and colonial ties all carry large negative coefficients. An extensive-intensive margin decomposition shows this operates entirely through the intensive margin — consistent with bilateral enforcement coordination suppressing realized transactions on jurisdictionally familiar corridors.
